Vincent van Gogh: Films and Documentaries

    Films about Vincent van Gogh

  1. Lust for Life (1956)

      IMDb Rating: 7.4/10
      Rotten Tomatoes Rating:86%
      Year: 1956
      Description: This biographical film follows the life of Vincent van Gogh from his early days as a preacher to his tragic death. It focuses on his struggles with mental illness and his relationship with his brother Theo.
      Main Actors: Kirk Douglas (Vincent van Gogh), Anthony Quinn (Paul Gauguin), James Donald (Theo van Gogh)
      Director: Vincente Minnelli

  2. Vincent & Theo (1990)

      IMDb Rating: 6.9/10
      Rotten Tomatoes Rating:78%
      Year: 1990
      Description: This film explores the turbulent relationship between Vincent van Gogh and his brother Theo, highlighting Vincent's struggles with his art and mental health, and Theo's attempts to support his brother.
      Main Actors: Tim Roth (Vincent van Gogh), Paul Rhys (Theo van Gogh)
      Director: Robert Altman

  3. At Eternity's Gate (2018)

      IMDb Rating: 6.9/10
      Rotten Tomatoes Rating:79%
      Year: 2018
      Description: This biographical drama focuses on the last years of Vincent van Gogh's life, exploring his artistic genius and the struggles he faced with his mental health.
      Main Actors: Willem Dafoe (Vincent van Gogh), Rupert Friend (Theo van Gogh), Oscar Isaac (Paul Gauguin)
      Director: Julian Schnabel

    Documentaries about Vincent van Gogh

  1. Vincent: The Life and Death of Vincent van Gogh (1987)

      IMDb Rating: 7.5/10
      Rotten Tomatoes Rating:83%
      Year: 1987
      Description: This documentary offers an in-depth look at the life of Vincent van Gogh, using his letters to provide a voiceover narrative that chronicles his struggles and achievements.
      Main Actors: John Hurt (Narrator)
      Director: Paul Cox

  2. Van Gogh: Painted with Words (2010)

      IMDb Rating: 7.9/10
      Year: 2010
      Description: This docudrama, starring Benedict Cumberbatch as Vincent van Gogh, dramatizes the artist's life using his letters as the basis for the script, providing a unique and intimate portrayal.
      Main Actors: Benedict Cumberbatch (Vincent van Gogh)
      Director: Andrew Hutton
